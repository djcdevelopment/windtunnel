Autoreason × Wind-Tunnel — Crossover Map

Companion note · 2026-07-10 · what in the Matrix Wind-Tunnel speaks to NousResearch/autoreason PR #2 ("bilevel autoreason: an outer meta-loop that adapts the inner loop's search procedure between runs", @thedavidmurray). Highlights the PR's interest areas against our six rounds of idle-hardware data, and hands over the calibration.

One-paragraph version

The bilevel PR builds a deterministic outer loop that reads a tournament trace, diagnoses one of six failure signatures with a rule-based classifier (no LLM at the diagnosis step), applies a mechanism change, and keeps it only if it passes a quality gate and reduces rounds-to-converge. The wind-tunnel is, in effect, a controlled study of the one thing that gate depends on and treats as reliable: the judge. Our headline result — a single judge rubric inflated every effect ~3× and could flip a decision's sign — is direct empirical backing for the PR's "diversify judge lenses" mechanism, and it sizes the gate: on our numbers a 1-vote gate is hopeless. Below: his six signatures against our data, his mechanism library against our measured deltas, and the gate math. The runnable apparatus that produced these numbers is in apparatus/; the proof run is APPARATUS-PROOF.html.

1 · His thesis is our doctrine

The PR narrows to a deterministic diagnosis "rather than generative LLM-based mechanisms for auditability and cost efficiency" — no model call in the outer loop. That is the wind-tunnel's two-economies doctrine almost verbatim: run the cheap, sunk-compute controller on idle local hardware; reserve metered frontier tokens for the inner generation that actually needs them.

  • His outer loop = Economy 2 (sunk, marginal cost ≈ 0). His inner tournament = the metered leg.
  • His "cost efficiency" is an asserted motivation with no benchmark yet. Measuring knowledge-per-local-hour is exactly what the wind-tunnel is built to do — so we can turn his motivation into a number.

2 · His six signatures × our evidence

The PR's classifier routes a trace into one of six bottleneck signatures. Our runs are a live instance of several of them — and quantify two that are pure judge pathologies.

autoreason signaturefitwhat the wind-tunnel saw
non_convergence DIRECT Critic emitted VERDICT: CONVERGED on 0/12 pilot cells; every cell burned its full lap budget. Our trace is this signature. Finding: the lap cap, not convergence, is the real control knob.
judge_herding DIRECT + CALIBRATED Re-judged 192 finals under 3 rubrics. The completeness lens herds — arm spread collapses to 1.3 pts, it can't tell the arms apart. Default over-separates (9.9); neutral sits at 2.9. We can hand over the base rates.
oscillation PARTIAL baseline (default judge) goes 85.2 / 84.2 / 84.3 / 74.5 — a blip then a cliff. Under the neutral judge the same series is a mild −3.7 drift, no cliff: much of the "bounce" was the judge, not the loop.
position_bias NEEDS A BUILD Not applicable yet: our judge scores each response absolutely (0–100, one at a time) — there is no A/B position to bias. autoreason's tournament is pairwise, where it's real. Studying it here needs a pairwise-judge mode added first (our ordering axis swaps the planner/critic model, a different, model-confounded thing).
premature_convergence OPPOSITE Didn't fire — we never converged at all (the opposite failure). Honest: our loop's problem is too many laps, not too few.
candidate_collapse HIS TURF Not measurable here — the wind-tunnel runs a single refine chain per cell, not a candidate population. This is autoreason's home ground; a wind-tunnel population arm would be needed to light it up.

Score: 2 of 6 direct (one with full calibration), 1 partial, 3 not-yet (one needs a pairwise-judge build, two are honestly out of our scope). The two direct hits — non_convergence and judge_herding — are the ones his classifier would fire most on a self-refinement loop, and they're exactly where we have data.

3 · His mechanism library × our measured deltas

The PR maps signatures to "concrete config deltas — escalating critic difficulty, diversifying judge lenses, adjusting temperature, tuning convergence thresholds." We ran two of those mechanisms and measured what they actually do to planning quality.

mechanism (his)wind-tunnel measurement
diversify judge lenses
(for judge_herding)		 Backed. 3 rubrics over 192 finals. The consensus (neutral) lens is the honest ruler; single lenses inflate up to 2.86× and one flips the sign. His instinct is right, and we can hand him the calibration to set --gate-votes.
escalate critic difficulty
(for premature_convergence)		 Caution — measured backfire. Our thorough-critic arm is this mechanism (demand exhaustive coverage). It was the worst arm: 80.2 default, −0.1 vs baseline even on the neutral judge. Escalating the critic degraded planning at every lap.
tune convergence thresholds / cap laps
(for non_convergence)		 Backed, and nearly free. Under a fair judge, scores are flat ~87–92 across laps 1–4, so a hard lap cap costs ≈0 quality while bounding compute. Capping L4→L2 removes the default judge's −10.7 collapse.
(not in his library yet)
concise author prompt		 A lever to add. "Shortest complete, buildable answer; lead with the decision." The single robust win across all six rounds: +2.8 vs baseline (neutral judge), flat-high ~91 across laps 1–6, immune to over-refinement. An author-side mechanism his inner loop could adopt directly.

4 · His gate × our judge-robustness math the load-bearing contribution

The outer loop keeps a change only if it "passes a quality gate (majority vote across --gate-votes trials), then rounds-to-converge becomes the optimization objective." That gate is judged. If the judge is biased, the hill-climb ascends a biased gradient. The wind-tunnel's Round 6 gives the gate the numbers it needs to not chase noise. For concise_author vs baseline:

quantityvaluewhat it means for the gate
effect by lens+8.0 / −0.2 / +2.8default / completeness / neutral — same artifacts, three answers
consensus effect+2.8the honest effect size (neutral lens)
single-lens inflation2.86×a lucky lens exaggerates the win nearly 3×
between-lens noise4.15 pts> the effect itself — one lens can dominate the signal
sign-flip lensescompletenessa 1-vote gate drawing this lens would revert a real gain
required gate votes≈ 9to resolve +2.8 against 4.15-pt noise at ~2σ

The takeaway for the PR: --gate-votes 1 (or a single lens) is not merely weak here — it has a one-in-three chance of reverting a genuine improvement. The wind-tunnel says the gate needs roughly a dozen lens-diverse votes before "quality passed" means anything on a self-refinement task with a ~3-point effect. That is the empirical floor his gate should be sized against.

5 · What we can run together

  • Supply the empirical section the PR is missing. Its own status line: "✗ Empirical benefit unproven; no benchmark results included." We have the harness (matrix generator, held-out judge panel, multi-rubric re-judge) and idle B70/OMEN compute to wire run_bilevel.py's outer loop over a wind-tunnel inner tournament and produce that benchmark.
  • Harden his gate with our re-judge. Port the 3-rubric variance check to his quality gate; if effects collapse under re-judging like ours did, his rounds-to-converge signal is partly instrument noise.
  • Add a pairwise-judge mode. Our judge scores absolutely; his tournament is pairwise. A pairwise mode is the prerequisite to (a) test whether our mechanisms survive his scoring regime and (b) give his position_bias signature a real base rate.
  • Joint note: "The judge is the confound in self-improving LLM loops" — his control architecture (diversify lenses, blind pairwise gate) + our controlled measurement (~3× inflation). Two halves of one insight.
