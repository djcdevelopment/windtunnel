What this proves

The autoreason PR argues the outer loop should be deterministic — "a rule-based classifier over the trace ... with no LLM call at the diagnosis step" — for auditability and cost. This apparatus is that classifier, pointed at real consumer-hardware data. It (1) scans for all six of his failure signatures, (2) picks the highest-severity one, (3) looks up the mechanism bundle seeded with our measured deltas, and (4) sizes his quality gate from our judge-robustness numbers. Everything below came out of python run_apparatus.py — no tokens spent, which is the whole point.

How to read it

  • Step 1 shows the wind-tunnel trace is a real instance of his non_convergence signature, and a live instance of judge_herding under the completeness lens — the two signatures a self-refinement loop trips most.
  • Step 2 returns a config bundle carrying an empirical prior, not a guess: our runs already measured that the lap cap is nearly free and that the concise-author prompt is the robust lever.
  • Step 3 is the load-bearing part. It turns "diversify the judge" into a number: with a ~+2.8 effect and ~4.15-pt between-lens noise, his gate needs ≈9 lens-diverse votes, and a 1-vote gate has a one-in-three chance of reverting a genuine gain (the completeness lens flips the sign).

This is the diagnosis half of his outer loop, proven runnable on real data at zero token cost. The other half — actually applying a mechanism and re-running the inner tournament on B70/OMEN to produce the empirical benefit his PR flags as missing — is the joint experiment we can stand up next.
